ORIGINS

From as early as I can remember, I was hustling. From selling waters and sodas at garage sales as a child to becoming a Youtube Partner at the age of 13, entrepreneurship has always called to me.

At age 13, I was getting paid a couple hundred bucks a month for my Youtube gaming videos. This was also the time I started dabbling in Adobe applications like Photoshop and After Effects. I met a lot of people through that Youtube experience, and managed to even garner clients for freelance graphic design work.

Not only was I making money through Youtube, but I was getting paid to design logos, Youtube backgrounds, headers, and more for other Youtubers in the space.

RYNO'S TEES

My next big step in entreprenuerialism was opening up my own ecommerce clothing store, called Ryno's Tees, at the age of 18.

I was always messing around in Adobe applications and one day I decided to put my designs on some tees and get them printed. A couple friends told me they wanted to buy the tees off me, so I decided why not open up a store online, and see if other people around the world would want to buy my designs too.

I got my first online sale from a random person within the first few days of my products being up on Etsy. Fastforward to today, and this shop has managed to get over 1,000 sales from all over the world bringing in almost $50,000 in revenue.

GOOFY BOARDING CO.

Piggybacking off of Ryno's Tees, at the age of 22 I decided I wanted to start my own skateboard brand, where I would design all sorts of skateboard gear and sell it online and locally.

I've been successfully running this brand myself for over 4 years now. I do things like design the products, package and shipping, create marketing content, run socials, and quite frankly everything else involved in running a business.

The brand has gained traction across Illinois and is still expanding outwards. I've sold products in reputable skate shops as well as online and in-person, managing to bring in thousands of sales.

SK8 RATZ

When I first learned about NFTs, I felt like it was right in my wheelhouse. The technology was new to me, and I knew it would be difficult to create an entire NFT project myself, but I was determined to see it through on the chance my project finds success.

I created and launched SK8 RATZ NFTs in 2022, using online digital marketing to create buzz and generate sales. On launch day the sales started rolling in, but unfortunately the project never fully minted out.

Although the project can still be looked at as a success, it didn't turn out how I had hoped/expected. Fortunately, the failures along the way taught me more than anything.
